Kethematics is a proposed new field of study that examines the evolving role of family in human history, particularly in the age of Artificial Intelligence (AI). This interdisciplinary field aims to explore how family dynamics shape individuals, societies, and cultures, and how the structure and function of family units have changed over time. 1. What Does Kethematics Study?

Kethematics focuses on the relationship between family dynamics and individual and societal development, particularly in the context of modern technological changes. It seeks to answer key questions such as:

How does the advent of AI affect family functioning, communication, and intergenerational relationships?

Unlike psychology or sociology, Kethematics does not merely analyze the individual or the broader society; it uniquely investigates the central role of family as both a structural and emotional unit, examining how it interacts with external factors such as technology and social trends.

While Kethematics aims to provide a deeper understanding of family dynamics and its role in shaping individuals and societies, like any emerging field, it also comes with certain risks and potential negative consequences. These include:

As Kethematics seeks to provide insights into societal issues through the lens of family dynamics, there is a potential danger that these insights could be misused in policy-making.
